The Quiet Power of Local AI

By Jane Doe · Example Times

Running machine-learning models directly on a user's device used to be impractical. The models were too large, the runtimes too slow, and the browser APIs too immature. Over the last two years that has changed dramatically.

Why it matters

When inference happens locally, no data ever leaves the machine. There is no server to bill you by the token, no audio uploaded to a third party, and no account required.

The trade-off is that the device must do the work. Modern laptops and phones are surprisingly capable, especially with hardware acceleration through WebGPU.

Quality has improved too. Small neural voices now sound natural enough for long-form listening, closing much of the gap with cloud services.
