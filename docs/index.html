Audiofi Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 19, 2026

Audiofi reads the article you're looking at out loud, on your own device. There is no Audiofi server. We have no accounts, no analytics, and no trackers, and we never receive your data.

What Audiofi handles

Page content. When you ask Audiofi to read a page, it takes that page's text (or, for a PDF, its contents) and turns it into audio in your browser. The text is kept only for the current browsing session and is dropped when you close the browser. It is not sent to us or to anyone else.

Your settings. Voice, speed, volume, and light/dark choice are saved on your device with the browser's local storage so they stick between sessions. They stay on your device.

The voice model. The first time you play something, Audiofi downloads the Kokoro voice model from Hugging Face, and the browser keeps it cached. From then on, speech is produced offline. Apart from reading the page you point it at, this is the only network request Audiofi makes.

What Audiofi does not do

  • No sign-in and no personal information.
  • No analytics, telemetry, advertising, or tracking.
  • No data sold or shared — nothing leaves your device to sell or share.
  • No work in the background. Audiofi touches a page only when you tell it to read one.

Permissions

Audiofi asks for the access it needs to read a page on demand and play it back. No permission is used to collect or transmit your data:

  • Read and change data on the sites you visit — to extract the text of a page you ask it to read, and to detect and load PDFs served without a .pdf address. Used only when you start reading a page.
  • Side panel — to show the reading view and playback controls.
  • Storage — to remember your voice, speed, volume, and theme, and to hold the current session's text. Cleared when the browser closes.
  • Offscreen document — to run speech synthesis, which needs audio playback the background script can't provide.
