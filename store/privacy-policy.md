# Audiofi Privacy Policy

_Last updated: June 19, 2026_

Audiofi reads the article you're looking at out loud, on your own device. There is
no Audiofi server. We have no accounts, no analytics, and no trackers, and we never
receive your data.

## What Audiofi handles

**Page content.** When you ask Audiofi to read a page, it takes that page's text (or,
for a PDF, its contents) and turns it into audio in your browser. The text is kept
only for the current browsing session and is dropped when you close the browser. It
is not sent to us or to anyone else.

**Your settings.** Voice, speed, volume, and light/dark choice are saved on your
device with the browser's local storage so they stick between sessions. They stay on
your device.

**The voice model.** The first time you play something, Audiofi downloads the Kokoro
voice model from Hugging Face, and the browser keeps it cached. From then on, speech
is produced offline. Apart from reading the page you point it at, this is the only
network request Audiofi makes.

## What Audiofi does not do

- No sign-in and no personal information.
- No analytics, telemetry, advertising, or tracking.
- No data sold or shared — nothing leaves your device to sell or share.
- No work in the background. Audiofi touches a page only when you tell it to read one.

## Permissions

Audiofi asks for the access it needs to read a page on demand and play it back.
Each permission and the reason for it is listed in `store/listing.md`. None of them
are used to collect or transmit your data.
